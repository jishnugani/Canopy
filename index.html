Climate Change

Global warming poses a significant threat to Nigeria, the most populous country in Africa. One of the most noticeable effects is the rise in temperatures, leading to more intense heatwaves and disrupting agricultural cycles. This heat stress negatively affects crop yields, diminishes water resources, and contributes to the spread of diseases, placing immense pressure on food security and public health. Additionally, Nigeria's vast coastline faces increasing risks from rising sea levels. Coastal erosion, saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources, and more severe flooding events threaten homes and livelihoods in heavily populated coastal areas..

Effects of Trees

Trees play a vital role in mitigating the effects of global warming in Nigeria. As natural carbon sinks, trees absorb carbon dioxide (CO2), a major greenhouse gas, from the atmosphere. This process helps reduce the heat-trapping effects that contribute to rising global temperatures. By planting trees, Nigeria can make a significant contribution to reducing its carbon footprint and slowing the pace of climate change..

How can I help?

Want to make a difference in your community and combat climate change? Tree planting is a fantastic way to get involved! Planting trees provides shade, cleans the air, and creates habitats for wildlife. You don't need a huge backyard to participate. Look for local organizations that host tree-planting events. These groups often provide the trees, tools, and guidance, making it easy to lend a hand..
